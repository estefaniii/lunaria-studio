The playbook for tripling sales in 90 days

By Estéfani Torres

Picture a typical small boutique: it sells nicely on Instagram, but every month it gets harder to land new customers. It depends 100% on the algorithm. Its website is a generic template almost nobody visits, and it posts whenever it remembers to. Sound familiar?

This article is a playbook: the step-by-step process we apply when a brand in this exact situation asks for help. It's not one specific case — it's the pattern we see repeat itself, and the methodology that unlocks it. The goal: triple online sales in roughly 90 days, with discipline and no gimmicks.

The starting point they all share

When we run an initial audit on a boutique at this stage, the findings are surprisingly similar:

  • Inconsistent visual identity: the Instagram logo doesn't match the one on the shopping bag, photos follow no pattern, colors change from post to post.
  • A website with no purpose: three or four generic sections, no clear call to action, no payment gateway integrated.
  • Reactive content: posts whenever there's time, with no editorial calendar or strategy behind them.
  • Zero measurement: no Google Analytics, no Meta Pixel, no dashboard telling them what works and what doesn't.

"It felt like running on a treadmill — a lot of effort, same place." We hear this line almost every day.

The challenge, then, isn't to work harder. It's to change the system — to go from having a digital presence to having a digital ecosystem. And that's built in four clear stages.

Stage 1 · Branding (weeks 1–3)

Before the logo, before the colors, before a single pixel — we define the soul of the brand. Discovery sessions to clarify:

  • Brand archetype (is it the creator? the sage? the lover?)
  • Voice and tone (casual, formal, playful, premium)
  • A concrete promise (what happens to the customer when they buy)
  • Positioning vs. competitors

From there comes a coherent visual system: editorial typography, a strategic (not decorative) color palette, a photography system, and a complete brand manual. Logo variants, digital stationery, IG templates, mockups.

Key takeaway Branding isn't decoration — it's strategy made visible. Before the logo, the promise. Before the colors, the ideal customer. Skip this, and everything else limps along.

Stage 2 · A website that sells (weeks 4–6)

With the brand defined, next comes the house. The tool matters less than the architecture: for boutiques we usually use a lightweight Shopify setup or a custom site with its own domain. What we never compromise on:

  • A hero section with an emotional headline and a single primary CTA.
  • A featured product with storytelling, not just a price tag.
  • Visible social proof: real testimonials with name and photo.
  • A visible return policy: it lowers the cognitive friction of buying online.
  • Local plus international payment gateways: a local option for domestic customers, Stripe and PayPal for international ones.
  • Tracking from day one: GA4, Meta Pixel and conversion events configured before launch.

Without data there are no decisions — only opinions. That's why measuring is the first principle.

Stage 3 · Digital management (weeks 7–12)

With the brand and the new website in place, next comes the traffic engine. We structure content using the 40/30/20/10 rule:

  • 40% educational — tips, advice, hacks that build authority.
  • 30% social proof — before and afters, testimonials, case studies.
  • 20% behind the scenes — process, day-to-day, mood boards.
  • 10% conversion — offers, drops, special bundles.

In volume, for a typical boutique this translates to 12 monthly feed posts, 4 reels, and daily stories coordinated with an editorial calendar. Meta ads segmented by interest (fashion, lifestyle, women 25–45, geo-targeted) with creatives running weekly A/B tests. We start with $50 a month and scale based on performance.

Results it's reasonable to expect

When this playbook is executed with discipline, the typical ranges at the 90-day mark are:

  • Online sales growth between 2x and 3x versus the previous quarter.
  • Website traffic multiplied by 4x or more with basic SEO done right.
  • Cost per lead cut in half or more, thanks to winning creatives and better targeting.
  • Average order value rising between 15% and 30% thanks to better visual hierarchy and natural cross-selling.
  • Return rate dropping several points, thanks to honest photos and a clear sizing guide.

We don't promise exact numbers because they depend on the product, market, budget and execution. But those are the ranges we see when the full system is applied.

The lesson that matters

Branding without a website is vanity. A website without digital management is a flyer on an empty street. Digital management without branding is noise forgotten in a scroll.

All three together, in this order, executed with coherence — that's where the magic happens. What we deliver isn't "more Instagram" — it's an ecosystem designed to sustain itself and keep producing results months after delivery.
